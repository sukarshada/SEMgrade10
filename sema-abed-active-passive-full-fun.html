Active and Passive Voice Master Class

A bigger, fuller, more challenging one-hour lesson for smart 10th grade EFL students. The class starts from the beginning, then moves into deeper practice with many activities: noticing, grammar focus, identification, transformation, error correction, meaning focus, long passive voice question sets, and a final writing challenge.

Part 1

Start from the Beginning

Active Voice

The scientist discovered the cure.

The subject (the scientist) does the action.

Pattern: Subject + Verb + Object

Passive Voice

The cure was discovered by the scientist.

The subject (the cure) receives the action.

Pattern: Object + be + past participle (+ by + subject)

Why passive?

  • to focus on the action
  • to focus on the result
  • when the doer is unknown

Be verbs

  • am / is / are
  • was / were
  • will be

Past participles

  • write → written
  • make → made
  • discover → discovered
Part 2

Noticing Task: What Changes?

The police arrested the thief.

The thief was arrested by the police.

Discuss

  • Which sentence starts with the doer?
  • Which sentence starts with the receiver?
  • Which one sounds more formal?
  • Which one focuses on the result?

Teacher prompt

Ask students why newspapers, reports, and formal writing often use the passive.

Part 3

Activity A: Active or Passive?

Choose the correct answer for each sentence.

1. The windows were cleaned yesterday.

2. The students finished the project.

3. A new hospital will be opened next year.

4. My father drives this car.

5. The letters were sent by the manager.

6. The chef prepares the food every morning.

Part 4

Activity B: Choose the Correct Passive Form

These are more challenging. Focus on the form very carefully.

1. They build houses in this town.

2. Someone stole my bike.

3. The company will announce the results tomorrow.

4. People speak Arabic in many countries.

Part 5

Activity C: Change Active into Passive

Write the passive sentence. These are open questions.

1. The students cleaned the classroom.

2. Shakespeare wrote Hamlet.

3. The school will organize a science fair.

4. People speak English all over the world.

5. The mechanic repaired the car.

Part 6

Part 7

Activity E: Meaning Focus

Choose the best answer. Think about why passive voice is used.

1. “The road was closed yesterday.” Why might passive be used here?

2. “The prize was won by Lina.” What is the focus?

3. “My phone was stolen.” Why is this passive useful?

Part 8

Long Passive Voice Question Set

These questions are for A1 learners, but they are made more challenging by using careful choices. There are 8 questions. Each correct answer = 5 points.

1. The book ______ by Sara every week.

2. The room ______ every day.

3. Choose the correct passive sentence.

4. The food ______ by my mother now.

5. A new bridge ______ next year.

6. The homework ______ by the students yesterday.

7. Choose the correct passive sentence.

8. English ______ in many countries.

Part 9

Mini Writing Challenge

Task A

Write two active sentences.

Task B

Change them into two passive sentences.

Use this as a speaking or writing task at the end of the lesson.

Part 10

Final Challenge

Answer the final question in your own words.

Turn this sentence into the passive: The workers build houses in this city.
